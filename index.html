About the Story

A Tale of Language, Loss & Patriotism

Set during the Franco-Prussian War of 1870–71, The Last Lesson follows young Franz, a schoolboy in Alsace who dreads his French lessons. One morning, he arrives late to school only to discover something heart-breaking — this is the last French lesson ever to be given. The Prussians have ordered all schools in Alsace and Lorraine to teach only German.

His devoted teacher, M. Hamel, dressed in his finest Sunday clothes, conducts the lesson with quiet dignity and profound sorrow. The village elders fill the back benches, silently mourning the loss of their language and culture.

The story closes with M. Hamel unable to speak, writing "Vive La France!" on the blackboard — his final, wordless act of defiance and love.
